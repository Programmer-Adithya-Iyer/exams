## Air pollution

### Core definitions and concepts
- **Pollution:** Undesirable change in the physical, chemical, or biological characteristics of any environmental component that harms life.
- **Pollutant:** Substance or energy present at the wrong concentration, place, or time (e.g., CO, SO2, NOx, PM, VOCs, noise, heat).
- **Primary pollutants:** Emitted directly (SO2, CO, NOx, PM, VOCs).
- **Secondary pollutants:** Formed in air via reactions (O3, HNO3, H2SO4, PANs, secondary PM; photochemical smog).

### Sources
- **Natural:** Volcanic eruptions, forest fires, sea salt aerosols, pollen, spores.
- **Anthropogenic:** Thermal power plants, industries (smelters, refineries, cement, textiles), fossil fuel combustion, vehicles, agriculture (ammonia), domestic solvents/paints.

### Pollutant-specific notes
- **CO (carbon monoxide):**
  - **Source:** Incomplete combustion (engines, stoves).
  - **Sink:** Soil microorganisms oxidize CO to CO2.
  - **Impact:** Forms carboxyhemoglobin; leads to hypoxia, neurological effects, coma.
  - **Control:** Engine and combustion optimization; catalytic converters (oxidation stage).

- **NOx (NO, NO2):**
  - **Formation:** High-temperature combustion; lightning; fixation pathways.
  - **Atmospheric chemistry:** Cycles between NO/NO2; forms HNO3, N2O5 (acid deposition); ozone formation in presence of VOCs and sunlight.
  - **Impacts:** Photochemical smog, acid rain, bronchitis.
  - **Controls:** Low-NOx burners; flue gas scrubbing; selective catalytic reduction (SCR) with NH3/urea; EGR in engines.

- **SOx (SO2, SO3):**
  - **Formation:** Sulfur in fuels; metal sulfide smelting; oxidation to SO3.
  - **Impacts:** Acid rain; material corrosion (CaCO3 â†’ CaSO4); plant chlorosis; respiratory issues.
  - **Controls:** Low-sulfur fuels; FGD (wet/dry scrubbers, limestone/lime); special scrubbers (e.g., citric acid); process fuel-switching.

- **Particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10, ultrafine):**
  - **Sources:** Combustion, dust, mining, industry, biomass burning.
  - **Impacts:** Respiratory and cardiovascular disease; carcinogen adsorption; material soiling; visibility reduction; climate radiative effects.
  - **Controls:** Gravity settling chambers; cyclone separators; filters (baghouse); electrostatic precipitators; wet scrubbers.

- **Hydrocarbons/VOCs (benzene, toluene, methane):**
  - **Sources:** Motor fuels, solvents, biogenic emissions.
  - **Impacts:** Photochemical smog precursors; carcinogenic/irritant effects; narcotic effects (methane at high conc).
  - **Controls:** Incineration/thermal oxidizers; adsorption (activated carbon); leak detection and repair (LDAR); low-VOC formulations.

### Signature phenomena
- **Photochemical smog (Los Angeles type):**
  - **Formation:** NOx + VOCs + sunlight â†’ O3, PANs, aldehydes; brown haze; eye irritation; plant damage.
  - **Controls:** Reduce NOx/VOCs; traffic control; vapor recovery; urban planning.

- **Classical smog (London type):**
  - **Formation:** High SO2 + PM in cool, humid air; reducing environment.
  - **Controls:** Desulfurization, clean fuels, emission control.

- **Acid rain:**
  - **Chemistry:** SO2/NOx â†’ H2SO4/HNO3 in air â†’ deposition.
  - **Impacts:** Aquatic acidification; soil nutrient leaching; forest damage; building corrosion.
  - **Controls:** SO2/NOx reduction; liming lakes; emissions trading schemes.

- **Global warming (greenhouse gases: CO2, CH4, N2O, CFCs):**
  - **Impacts:** Sea-level rise; climate shifts; agriculture stress; extreme events.
  - **Mitigation:** Energy efficiency; renewables; reforestation; phase-down HFCs.

- **Ozone layer depletion (stratosphere):**
  - **Mechanism:** Halocarbon-derived Cl/Br catalyze O3 destruction.
  - **Impacts:** UV-B increase; skin cancer, cataracts; ecosystem damage.
  - **Solutions:** Montreal Protocol; shift to HFCs/HFOs (with low ODP; watch GWP).

### Measurement and standards
- **AQI (Air Quality Index):** Composite index of PM2.5/PM10, O3, NO2, SO2, CO; health bands from good to hazardous.
- **Stack monitoring:** Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) for SOx/NOx/PM.

### Control technologies quick reference
- **Particulates:** Gravity settler (coarse), cyclone (coarse), baghouse (fine), ESP (fine), wet scrubber (gas + PM).
- **Gases:** Wet scrubbers (SO2), SCR/SNCR (NOx), catalytic oxidation (CO/VOCs).

## Water pollution

### Definitions and types
- **Water pollution:** Contamination making water unfit for intended use (drinking, irrigation, ecology).
- **Types:**
  - **Surface water pollution:** Rivers, lakes, wetlandsâ€”point and non-point inputs.
  - **Groundwater pollution:** Infiltration of contaminants (nitrate, pesticides, heavy metals).
  - **Chemical pollution:** Industrial discharges, oil spills, solvents.
  - **Microbiological pollution:** Pathogens (bacteria, protozoa, viruses).
  - **Nutrient pollution (eutrophication):** Excess N&P â†’ algal bloom â†’ DO drop.
  - **Suspended matter:** Siltation, turbidity limiting light.
  - **Thermal pollution:** Hot discharge lowering DO; see thermal section.
  - **Radiological pollution:** Radioisotopes (medical, power).

### Sources
- **Point sources:** Sewage outfalls; industrial discharge pipes; power plants.
- **Non-point sources:** Agricultural runoff (fertilizers, pesticides), mining runoff, urban stormwater, acid rain.
- **Special sources:** Oil spills; landfill leachate; leaking USTs; poorly maintained septic systems.

### Key pollutants
- **Organic load:** BOD/COD drivers (sewage, food processing).
- **Nutrients:** Nitrates, phosphates (fertilizers, detergents).
- **Metals:** Pb, Hg, Cd, Cr (industrial).
- **Pathogens:** Fecal coliforms, cholera, typhoid agents.
- **Emerging:** Microplastics, PFAS, pharmaceuticals.

### Metrics and tests
- **BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand):** Oxygen used by microbes to degrade organics (5-day test). Lower than COD.
- **COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand):** Oxygen equivalent of chemically oxidizable matter (strong oxidant; short test). Higher than BOD.
- **DO (Dissolved Oxygen):** Oxygen available to aquatic life; typically >5 mg/L for healthy systems.
- **TDS/TSS:** Dissolved/ suspended solids.
- **pH, temperature, turbidity, conductivity:** Basic physico-chemical parameters.

### Eutrophication
- **Process:** Nutrient enrichment â†’ algal bloom â†’ bacterial decomposition â†’ DO depletion â†’ fish kills â†’ sediment build-up.
- **Controls:** Nutrient source control (agri BMPs), tertiary treatment (N/P removal), buffer strips, wetland restoration.

### Control and treatment
- **Source control:** Cleaner production; recycling; stormwater management; agro BMPs (contour farming, precision fertilization).
- **Wastewater treatment:**
  - **Primary:** Screening, grit removal, sedimentation, coagulation-flocculation.
  - **Secondary (biological):** Activated sludge (aeration + clarifier), trickling filters, oxidation ponds.
  - **Tertiary:** Filtration/microstraining, adsorption (activated carbon), nutrient removal (nitrification/denitrification; chemical precipitation of P), disinfection (chlorine, ozone, UV), odor control.
- **Reuse:** Treated effluent for cooling, irrigation (fit-for-purpose quality).
- **Radiological:** Segregation and specialized dewatering; controlled storage/disposal.

## Soil pollution

### Definitions, sources, and types
- **Soil pollution:** Toxic chemicals present at harmful concentrations; altered physicochemical properties harming biota, productivity.
- **Sources:**
  - **Domestic waste:** Plastic, metal, glass, organic refuse; pathogens from excreta.
  - **Industrial waste:** Textiles, tannery, electroplating, distillery, petroleum, cement.
  - **Agriculture:** Excess fertilizers (nitrates), pesticides (persistent organics like DDT).
  - **Biomedical waste:** Improper disposal.
  - **Radioactive:** Fallout, lab discharges; natural radionuclides.
  - **Solid waste mismanagement:** Open dumping; landfill leaks.

### Impacts
- **Human health:** Methemoglobinemia (blue baby syndrome) from nitrate; heavy metal toxicity; pathogen exposure.
- **Ecosystem:** Soil infertility; altered pH and nutrient cycles; groundwater contamination; biota poisoning; fluorosis from contaminated produce.
- **Air:** Odor and toxic gas emissions from dumps.

### Controls
- **Source reduction (4Rs):** Refuse, Reduce, Recover, Recycle.
- **Treatment:** Bioremediation (bacteria/fungi/yeasts), phytoremediation; composting biodegradable waste; safe incineration of non-biodegradable hazardous fractions.
- **Afforestation:** Stabilize and prevent erosion.
- **Sanitation:** Improve practices; segregate waste streams.
- **Regulation:** Enforce stringent pollution-control legislation; monitor landfill liners and leachate collection.

## Noise pollution

### Basics
- **Noise:** Unwanted sound; measured in decibels (dB).
- **Common levels:** Threshold of hearing (~0 dB); conversation (~60 dB); aircraft takeoff (~120 dB); threshold of pain (~146 dB).

### Sources
- **Transport:** Road, rail, air traffic.
- **Industry:** Power generation, fabrication, assembly.
- **Public address systems:** Events, festivals.
- **Agriculture:** Tractors, pumps.
- **Defense:** Firearms, explosions.
- **Household:** Mixers, blenders, vacuum cleaners.
- **Construction, concerts, pets.**

### Effects
- **Physical:** Temporary threshold shift (TTS), permanent hearing loss (NIPTS).
- **Physiological:** Muscle strain, headaches, stress, cardiovascular strain.
- **Psychological:** Irritability, depression, fatigue, reduced efficiency, poor concentration.

### Controls
- **At source:** Quiet design; maintenance; mufflers; equipment enclosures; silencing.
- **Along path:** Acoustic barriers; increased distance/path length; vegetation belts.
- **At receiver:** Soundproof rooms; earplugs/earmuffs; behavioral controls (limit exposure).
- **Policy:** Zoning; honking bans near sensitive zones; enforce limits; regulate loudspeakers.

## Thermal pollution

### Definition and sources
- **Thermal pollution:** Waste heat raising/lowering ambient water temperature, degrading water quality.
- **Key sources:** Power plant condensers; refineries, steel mills, nuclear plants; domestic sewage; deforestation (solar warming).

### Effects
- **Lower DO:** Warm water holds less O2; stresses aquatic life.
- **Physicochemical changes:** Density/viscosity; gas solubility.
- **Biological damage:** Enzyme activity impaired (>37â„ƒ); reproduction interference; metabolic rate increase; higher mortality; malnutrition via enzyme denaturation.
- **Cold releases:** Alter fauna; eliminate native species.

### Controls
- **Cooling ponds:** Evaporation/convection/radiation.
- **Cooling towers:** Wet (evaporative) and dry (air over pipes).
- **Cogeneration:** Reuse waste heat for district heating/industrial processes.
- **Stormwater management:** Bioretention to temper thermal pulses.
- **Afforestation:** Stream shading; reduce erosion and turbidity (lower heat absorption).

## Radiation pollution (nuclear hazards)

### Sources
- **Natural:** Cosmic radiation; terrestrial radionuclides (radon, uranium, thorium).
- **Anthropogenic:** Mining/processing of radioactive ores; nuclear power plants; medical/industrial isotopes; nuclear weapons tests; accidents.

### Effects
- **Somatic (exposed individual):** Acute (â‰¥10 RAD) hair loss, burns; delayed cataracts, cancers.
- **Genetic (heritable):** Mutations affecting future generations.
- **Environmental:** Soil infertility; biomagnification of radionuclides; cellular destruction; ozone impacts; nuclear winter (soot blocking sunlight).

### Controls
- **Siting:** Plants far from populations with buffer zones.
- **Safety:** Strict enforcement; training; redundancy; containment integrity.
- **Waste management:**
  - **HLW:** Vitrify to ceramics; deep geological disposal/salt mines.
  - **MLW:** Solidify with concrete; steel drums; deep burial/sea-bed chambers (with stringent safeguards).
  - **LLW:** Steel drums; concrete-lined trenches; site monitoring; drill bans.
- **Labeling and transport:** Clear hazard labels; radiation-proof containers; secure logistics.
- **Policy:** Ban tests; alternative energy; reuse in reactors where feasible.

## Disaster management

### What is a disaster?
- **Definition:** Sudden, calamitous event disrupting community/society, causing losses beyond local coping capacity. Formula insight: Disaster ~ (Vulnerability + Hazard) / Capacity.

### Classification
- **Natural:** Earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts, landslides, avalanches, forest fires.
- **Man-made:** Industrial accidents, pollution, fires, epidemics, wars, deforestation, riots.

### The 4Rs cycle
- **Response (during):** Public warnings; emergency ops; search & rescue; relief (food, medical); rapid assessments; evacuation.
- **Recovery (after):** Rehabilitation; reconstruction; temporary housing; insurance processing; long-term medical care; counseling.
- **Reduction/Mitigation (pre):** Building codes; zoning; vulnerability analysis; public education; retrofitting critical infrastructure.
- **Readiness/Preparedness (pre):** Emergency plans; training and drills; early warning systems; emergency communications.

### Hazard-specific essentials
- **Floods:**
  - **Causes:** Heavy rain; poor drainage; dam/embankment design; channel blockage; silting; cyclones; glacier melt; tides.
  - **Controls:** Dams/dikes/canals; diversion structures; runoff storage; sewer upgrades; flood hazard maps (extent, depth, velocity).
  - **During:** Seek higher ground; avoid moving water; shut utilities; donâ€™t drive through floods.
  - **After:** Avoid floodwater; beware weakened roads; avoid downed lines; ensure water safety; disinfect; inspect utilities.

- **Earthquakes:**
  - **Terms:** Focus/hypocenter; epicenter; magnitude (seismograph); intensity (Mercalli/Richter).
  - **Hazards:** Ground shaking; landslides; liquefaction; surface rupture; secondary (tsunami, seiche, flooding, fire).
  - **Steps:** Dropâ€“Coverâ€“Hold On; secure space; plan communications; disaster supplies; insurance; post-event evacuate/help/prevent further injury.

- **Cyclones:**
  - **Formation:** Warm ocean (>26.5â„ƒ); rising moist air; low pressure; Coriolis rotation; eye-wall; storm surge.
  - **Damage:** Winds; storm surges; heavy rains; infrastructure failure; contamination; disease outbreaks.
  - **Doâ€™s & Donâ€™ts:** Maintain structures; secure debris; monitor official warnings; avert rumors; pre-stock supplies.

- **Landslides:**
  - **Causes:** Snow/glacier melt; groundwater rise; volcanic activity; vegetation loss; erosion; deforestation; blasting/mining.
  - **Actions:** Identify vulnerable areas; early warning; hazard mapping; construction restrictions; afforestation; terrace farming; rapid response teams.
  - **If imminent:** Evacuate with supplies/documents; contact authorities; warn neighbors; return only after inspection.

### Emergency preparedness (any disaster)
- **Survival kit:** 3â€“4 days of food; 9 L water per person; radio/torch/batteries; first-aid; essential meds; hygiene items.
- **Family plan:** Supplies location; escape routes; safe shelter spots; nearest civil defense center; rendezvous plan; authorized school pickup.
